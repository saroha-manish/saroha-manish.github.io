Insurance Churn Prediction & Retention Activation

Project Overview

This project predicts policyholder churn at renewal and turns risk scores into targeted retention actions. Using behavioral, payment, and claims features, we train classification models to estimate the probability of churn and surface next-best offers (e.g., auto-debit enrollment, payment frequency change, loyalty credits). Insights are published to dashboards/CRM so sales and service teams can prioritize outreach and reduce attrition.

Key Features

  • Risk Scoring: Per-policy churn probability and tiered risk bands (Low/Med/High).
  • Robust Modeling: Logistic Regression baseline with calibrated probabilities; XGBoost/Random Forest for higher recall at similar precision.
  • Imbalance Handling: Class weights / SMOTE and threshold tuning for recall-focused targeting.
  • Explainability: Top drivers via coefficients/feature importances and SHAP plots for high‑risk cohorts.
  • Operational Integration: Scores pushed to CRM and shown in Power BI with filters for region/segment/agent.
  • A/B Friendly: Buckets customers by uplift hypothesis to enable controlled retention experiments.

Technologies and Tools

  • Programming: Python
  • Modeling: scikit‑learn (Logistic Regression, Random Forest), XGBoost
  • Data Processing: Pandas, NumPy (feature pipelines, leakage guards)
  • Visualization & Ops: Power BI dashboards; exports/webhooks to CRM
  • Storage: SQL warehouse; CSV/Excel extracts for ops teams

Problem Statement

Churn is concentrated around renewal but difficult to prevent without early signals. Agents lack a prioritized list and often spend effort on customers unlikely to churn, missing those at genuine risk. We needed reliable, interpretable churn scores with clear actions to reduce attrition cost‑effectively.

Solution Approach

We assembled features from policy, billing, and service systems (tenure, premium, payment frequency, auto‑debit, late‑payments, claims, endorsements, complaints, channel, city tier). After leakage checks and stratified splits, we trained a calibrated Logistic Regression and a tuned XGBoost/Random Forest. We optimized precision‑recall trade‑offs via thresholding aligned to retention capacity and cost.

  • Feature Pipeline: missing‑value imputation, one‑hot encoding, scaling where needed.
  • Calibration: probability calibration for rank ordering stability across cohorts.
  • Thresholding: choose operating point (e.g., P(churn) ≥ 0.4) to maximize saved revenue per contact.
  • Activation: push high‑risk lists + reason codes to CRM; Power BI slices for agent/region.

Results

  • Model Quality: ROC‑AUC 0.90, F1 0.78, Recall 0.82 at tuned threshold.
  • Business Impact: Targeted retention reduced churn by 12–15% in pilot cohorts.
  • Operational Wins: Prioritized call lists and clear reason codes shortened outreach cycles.

Note: Metrics reflect a representative deployment; adjust with your environment’s validation.

Challenges

Addressed class imbalance, seasonal renewal spikes, potential data leakage from post‑renewal events, and cold‑start customers with short tenures. We iterated on sampling, regularization, and segmentation to maintain stability across regions.

Future Enhancements

  • Uplift Modeling: Target customers with highest treatment effect, not just highest risk.
  • Cost‑Aware Optimization: Thresholds based on contact cost vs. premium retained.
  • Realtime Scoring: Stream scores near renewal events for same‑day agent action.
  • Explainability at Scale: SHAP summaries by agent/region for coaching and QA.
