Landis+Gyr Project

Role: Data Scientist

  • Built a predictive system to monitor telecom network quality using MOS (Mean Opinion Score) estimation, addressing a major lack of visibility into user experience.
  • Analyzed key network metrics including jitter, latency, packet loss, and throughput across millions of data points to train supervised learning models.
  • Implemented and optimized multiple ML models (Random Forest, XGBoost, LSTM) to ensure robust and accurate predictions under varying network conditions.
  • Achieved 94% prediction accuracy for MOS scores, significantly improving the organization's ability to proactively monitor service quality.
  • Integrated real-time predictions into monitoring dashboards, allowing engineers to detect and act on degradations before user impact occurred.
  • Reduced customer complaints by 22% and improved SLA adherence through faster resolution times and data-driven network management decisions.
