Agent Attrition Prediction & Retention Activation

Project Overview

Designed and deployed a production-grade Agent Attrition Prediction Model to identify early disengagement risk. The system analyzes tenure, productivity momentum, and engagement signals to generate per-agent attrition probability scores, enabling proactive retention interventions and data-driven workforce planning.

Key Features

  • Attrition Risk Scoring: Generated probability scores with Low / Medium / High segmentation.
  • Early Tenure Monitoring: Focused on first 60–120 days, the highest attrition window.
  • Reason Codes: Identified key drivers such as low productivity velocity and engagement gaps.
  • Cohort-Based Analysis: Tracked attrition trends across onboarding batches and branches.
  • Manager Activation: Delivered prioritized high-risk lists for structured follow-ups.

Technologies and Tools

  • Programming: Python
  • Modeling: XGBoost / LightGBM / scikit-learn
  • Data Processing: Pandas, NumPy
  • Visualization: Power BI dashboards
  • Data Storage: SQL warehouse & AWS S3

Problem Statement

Early agent attrition leads to significant revenue leakage and wasted onboarding investment. Managers lacked structured visibility into which agents were likely to disengage. A predictive and interpretable system was required to detect attrition risk early and enable timely coaching interventions.

Solution Approach

Built a supervised classification framework using tenure alignment, early productivity indicators, and activity-based features. Implemented cohort-based validation to prevent data leakage and ensure stable performance across onboarding batches. Model outputs were converted into operational risk tiers and actionable reason codes for real-world deployment.

  • Feature Engineering: Tenure stage, productivity velocity, activity frequency.
  • Validation: Time-based cohort split for realistic evaluation.
  • Threshold Optimization: Balanced precision and recall for retention team capacity.
  • Deployment: Integrated scoring outputs into leadership dashboards.

Results & Impact

  • Model Performance: Achieved ROC-AUC of 0.85+ with stable validation across unseen cohorts.
  • Early Risk Detection: Identified ~70–75% of attrition cases within the first 3 months.
  • Retention Improvement: Contributed to a 10–15% reduction in early agent attrition during pilot rollout.
  • Manager Efficiency: Reduced unstructured follow-ups by 25%+ through prioritized risk lists.
  • Workforce Planning: Enabled cohort-level attrition forecasting for strategic hiring adjustments.

Challenges

Differentiating temporary low performance from genuine disengagement required careful feature engineering and validation. Cohort and regional variability was addressed through stable modeling and periodic performance monitoring.
