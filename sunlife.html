Agent Attrition Prediction Model

Project Overview

Designed and implemented a production-ready Agent Attrition Prediction Model to identify early disengagement risk. The system analyzes tenure, performance momentum, and engagement signals to generate per-agent attrition risk scores, enabling proactive retention interventions and data-driven workforce planning.

Key Features

  • Attrition Risk Scoring: Generated probability scores with Low / Medium / High risk segmentation.
  • Early Tenure Monitoring: Focused on first 60â€“120 days, the most critical attrition window.
  • Reason Codes: Identified key drivers such as low productivity momentum and engagement gaps.
  • Cohort-Based Analysis: Tracked attrition trends across onboarding batches and branches.
  • Manager Activation: Delivered prioritized risk lists for structured follow-ups.

Technologies and Tools

  • Programming: Python
  • Modeling: XGBoost / LightGBM / scikit-learn
  • Data Processing: Pandas, NumPy
  • Visualization: Power BI dashboards
  • Data Storage: SQL warehouse & AWS S3

Problem Statement

Early agent attrition significantly impacts revenue and onboarding investments. Managers lacked structured visibility into which agents were likely to disengage. A predictive and interpretable system was required to detect attrition risk early and enable timely coaching interventions.

Solution Approach

Built a supervised classification framework using tenure alignment, early performance indicators, and activity-based features. Implemented cohort-based validation to prevent data leakage and ensure stability. Converted model outputs into operational risk tiers and actionable reason codes for real-world deployment.

  • Feature Engineering: Tenure stage, productivity velocity, activity frequency.
  • Validation: Time-based cohort split for realistic performance evaluation.
  • Threshold Optimization: Balanced precision and recall for retention team capacity.
  • Deployment: Integrated scoring outputs into leadership dashboards.

Results

  • Early Risk Identification: Enabled detection of high-risk agents within the first few months.
  • Improved Retention Prioritization: Focused manager effort on agents with highest attrition probability.
  • Operational Adoption: Risk segmentation and reason codes improved leadership trust and usage.
  • Strategic Workforce Insight: Cohort-level analysis supported onboarding and staffing decisions.

Challenges

Differentiating temporary low momentum from genuine disengagement required careful feature design and validation. Cohort variability across regions and managers was addressed through stable modeling and continuous monitoring.
